Section 01 — Executive Dashboard

Executive Dashboard

Business purpose: A single screen a CEO or Operations Director opens every Monday morning to know, in under 60 seconds, whether the business is healthy, what changed, and what needs a decision this week — without opening a spreadsheet.

Business Purpose
Replace the Friday "send me the numbers" email with a live, always-current view of revenue, profit, churn, and risk.
Inputs
Monthly revenue, operational cost, new customers, churned customers, marketing spend, macro indicators (inflation, demand score).
AI Processing Logic
Calculates derived metrics (CAC, churn %, growth %, profit), classifies each month's risk tier, and writes a 1-paragraph executive alert when thresholds are breached.
Outputs
8 KPI cards, 3 trend charts, 1 auto-generated executive alert, monthly risk classification.
Decision Value
Cuts weekly reporting prep from ~2 hours of spreadsheet work to a 60-second glance — every week, automatically.

Section 02 — AI Alert Center

AI Alert Center

Business purpose: Surface only what requires executive attention — not every number, just the ones that moved enough to matter — ranked by priority so a busy CEO reads the top 2 and ignores the rest.

Business Purpose
Stop important signals (churn spike, demand shock) from getting buried in a monthly report nobody reads in full.
Inputs
Month-over-month deltas on revenue, churn, CAC, inflation, demand score against defined thresholds.
AI Processing Logic
If a metric crosses a defined threshold (e.g. churn >20%, inflation >6%), AI drafts a 2-sentence trigger explanation and a specific recommended action, tagged by priority.
Outputs
Ranked alert feed — 6 alerts generated this quarter, 4 High priority, 2 Medium.
Decision Value
Reduces time-to-detection of a demand shock from "noticed at quarter-end" to "flagged the week it happened."

Section 03 — Root Cause Analysis Engine

Root Cause Analysis Engine

Business purpose: When a number moves, executives don't just want to know that it moved — they want to know why, in a causal chain they can act on. This engine connects macro signals to business outcomes instead of leaving the CEO to guess.

Business Purpose
Turn "revenue dropped" into "revenue dropped because X caused Y which caused Z" — a chain leadership can intervene on.
Inputs
Macro layer (inflation, cost of living, demand score) + business layer (revenue, churn, CAC) for the same period.
AI Processing Logic
Correlates macro deltas against business deltas in the same month, ranks candidate causes by timing and magnitude, and constructs a plain-language causal chain.
Outputs
A traced causal chain per risk event, plus a confidence note on correlation vs causation.
Decision Value
Prevents leadership from treating a symptom (churn) when the root cause (macro demand shock) requires a different response.

Section 04 — Risk Monitoring System

Risk Monitoring System

Business purpose: Classify the overall state of the business each month into a single risk tier — Low / Medium / High — so leadership has a consistent, comparable signal month over month instead of re-interpreting raw numbers every time.

Business Purpose
Give the board a single, defensible risk rating per month instead of a wall of metrics they must interpret themselves.
Inputs
Churn %, growth %, CAC trend, inflation, demand score — same data feeding the dashboard.
AI Processing Logic
Each metric is scored against a risk band; the overall tier is the worst-performing dimension, not an average — so one severe risk can't be diluted by good news elsewhere.
Outputs
Monthly Low/Medium/High classification with a 2-line justification.
Decision Value
Creates an early-warning trail — a board reviewing 6 months of ratings can see deterioration before it becomes a crisis.

Section 05 — Business Health Score

Business Health Score

Business purpose: Compress every metric on this dashboard into one number — like a credit score for the business — so a CEO can track trajectory at a glance and a board can compare quarter to quarter without re-reading every chart.

Business Purpose
One number for trajectory tracking — "are we healthier than last month?" answered instantly.
Inputs
Growth %, churn %, CAC efficiency, profit margin, demand score — five weighted components.
AI Processing Logic
Each component normalised to 0–100, weighted by business priority (retention weighted highest), summed into a composite score.
Outputs
Composite score 0–100 plus component-level breakdown showing what's dragging the score down.
Decision Value
Identifies the single weakest lever (here: retention) so leadership knows exactly where to focus, not just that "something" is wrong.

Section 07 — Decision Simulation Center

Decision Simulation Center

Business purpose: Before committing budget, let leadership compare 2–3 concrete response strategies side by side using the business's own Q1 numbers as the baseline — turning "what should we do" into a scored comparison.

Business Purpose
Replace gut-feel budget decisions with a side-by-side comparison grounded in this quarter's real cost and conversion data.
Inputs
Q1 actuals (CAC, churn, marketing spend, revenue) as the baseline for each scenario's projection.
AI Processing Logic
Applies each scenario's spend/retention assumptions to the Q1 baseline trend to project Q2 revenue, profit, and churn under each strategy.
Outputs
3 scenario cards with projected outcomes and a recommended choice.
Decision Value
Makes trade-offs explicit before money is spent, not after a quarter is lost finding out a strategy didn't work.

Section 08 — What-If Analysis

What-If Analysis

Business purpose: Let a CFO or Operations Director ask "what if we cut churn to X%" or "what if inflation stays at Y%" and see the projected financial impact instantly — interactive sensitivity testing, not a static forecast.

Business Purpose
Test sensitivity of profit to the two levers leadership actually controls — churn and CAC — before locking a budget.
Inputs
User-adjustable churn % and CAC sliders; Q1 actuals as the fixed baseline.
AI Processing Logic
Recalculates projected customer count, revenue, and profit live as sliders move, using the same formulas that power the historical KPIs.
Outputs
Live-updating projected revenue, profit, and customer count for the adjusted scenario.
Decision Value
Answers "is a 5-point churn reduction worth a ₹15K retention budget?" with a number, not an opinion.

🟡 Data Quality Note — Found in Raw Dataset
OPS FINDING
The customer-level dataset contains inconsistent channel naming ("Instagram" vs "Insta") and a misspelling ("Refferal" vs "Referral"). This is exactly the kind of data quality issue the Operations Monitoring Layer is designed to catch before it silently fragments channel-level reporting.
Recommended Fix: Standardise channel taxonomy at data entry (dropdown, not free text) — a Phase 2 build item, see Section 12.
Section 11 — Action Tracking System

Action Tracking System

Business purpose: Close the loop. A recommendation that's approved but never tracked is just a slide nobody revisits. This system tracks every approved recommendation through to a measured outcome.

Business Purpose
Prevent "we decided to do X" from becoming "did we ever actually do X?" three months later.
Inputs
Approved recommendations from Section 06, with owner and target month assigned at approval time.
AI Processing Logic
Cross-references next month's actual KPIs against the action's stated goal to auto-flag whether the metric moved in the right direction.
Outputs
Status-tracked action list — Done / In Progress / Pending — with measured impact where available.
Decision Value
Creates accountability — and a track record proving which AI recommendations actually worked, building trust in the system over time.

Data Source & Methodology Note

Q1 2025 Customer-Level Dataset (12 records) Monthly Business Summary (Jan–Mar) Macroeconomic Indicators (Inflation, COL Index, Demand Score) Derived Metrics: CAC, Churn %, Growth %, Profit

All figures in this Command Center are calculated directly from the uploaded dataset. CAC = Marketing Spend ÷ New Customers. Churn % = Churned Customers ÷ New Customers (as defined in source data). Growth % = month-over-month revenue change. No figures are invented; all projections in Sections 07–08 are explicitly labelled as projections, not actuals.
